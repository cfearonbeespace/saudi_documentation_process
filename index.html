A Three-Phase Journey

Importing LoRaWAN gateways and sensors into Saudi Arabia is a structured process managed through the mandatory Saber platform. This journey involves three distinct phases, from initial technical preparation to final customs clearance. Successfully navigating these steps ensures your products meet regulatory standards and reach the market without delay.

Phase 1: Preparation & Technical Documentation

1. Compile Technical File

Manufacturer gathers all product specs, manuals, and schematics.

2. Obtain EU RED Test Reports

Crucial for CST approval, covering RF, EMC, and Safety standards.

3. Appoint Saudi Importer

A mandatory partner with a Commercial Registration is required.

Phase 2: Saber Platform & Product Certification (PCoC)

4. Importer Registers Products

Importer adds devices to the Saber platform using correct HS codes.

5. Apply for PCoC & CST Approval

Importer selects a CB and submits the technical files for review.

6. Issuance of Certificates

After successful review and payment, certificates are issued electronically.

Phase 3: Shipment & Customs Clearance (SCoC)

7. Prepare Shipment Documents

Manufacturer prepares the commercial invoice and packing list.

8. Request Shipment Certificate (SCoC)

For each shipment, the Importer requests an SCoC via Saber.

9. Final Customs Clearance

The SCoC is verified electronically at customs, releasing the shipment.

Understanding the Certificates

Certificate Validity Periods

The two main product-level certificates have different validity periods. The PCoC must be renewed annually, while the CST approval is valid for two years.

Certificate Per Shipment

The SCoC is transactional and must be issued for every single shipment you send, linking back to a valid PCoC.
